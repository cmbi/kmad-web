Disorder prediction

We first try to find your sequence in the D2P2 database of precomputed disorder predictions. If the sequence is not present there, the disorder prediction is performed with 4 different predictors: DISOPRED, SPINE-D, PSIPRED, and PreDisorder.


D2P2
Database of Disordered Protein Predictions

Oates,M.E., Romero,P., Ishida,T., Ghalwash,M., Mizianty,M.J., Xue,B., Dosztányi,S., Uversky,V.N., Obradovic,Z., Kurgan,L., Dunker,A.K., Gough,J. (2013). "D2P2: Database of Disordered Protein Predictions." Nucleic Acids Research 41 (D1):D508-D516

DISOPRED

Ward JJ, Sodhi JS, McGuffin LJ, Buxton BF and Jones DT (2004)
Prediction and functional analysis of native disorder in proteins from the three kingdoms of life
Journal of Molecular Biology , 337:635-645.

SPINE-D

Tuo Zhang, Eshel Faraggi, Bin Xue, A. Keith Dunker, Vladimir N. Uversky, Yaoqi Zhou. SPINE-D: Accurate prediction of short and long disordered regions by a single neural-network based method. Journal of Biomolecular Structure and Dynamics. 2012, 29 (4):799-813.

PSIPRED

Jones, D.T. (1999) Protein secondary structure prediction based on position-specific scoring matrices. Journal of Molecular Biology 292:195-202.

PreDisorder

X. Deng, J. Eickholt, and J. Cheng. PreDisorder: Ab Initio Sequence-Based Prediction of Protein Disordered Regions. BMC Bioinformatics, 10:436, 2009.
J. Hecker, J. Yang, and J. Cheng. Protein Disorder Prediction at Multiple Levels of Sensitivity and Specificity. BMC Genomics. 9(S1):S9, 2008.
J. Cheng, M. Sweredoski, and P. Baldi. Accurate Prediction of Protein Disordered Regions by Mining Protein Structure Data, Data Mining and Knowledge Discovery, vol. 11, no. 3, pp. 213-222, 2005.

KMAN
Knowledge Based Multiple Sequence Alignment for Natively Unfolded Proteins

The tool incorporates information from databases and predictions about functional sites to perform a Multiple Sequence Alignment (MSA). The information includes phosphorylations predicted by NetPhos, annotated posttranslational modifications (PTMs) from Uniprot, short linear motifs (SLiMs) from ELM, and functional domains predicted by Pfam.


ELM

Dinkel H., Van Roey K., Michael S., Davey N.E., Weatheritt R.J., Born D., Speck T., Krüger D., Grebnev G., Kubań M., Strumiłło M., Uyar B., Budd A., Altenberg B., Seiler M., Chemes L.B., Glavina J., Sánchez I.E., Diella F., Gibson T.J. The eukaryotic linear motif resource ELM: 10 years and counting Nucleic Acids Research (2013) Database issue 42:D259-266

NetPhos

Blom, N., Gammeltoft, S., and Brunak, S. Sequence- and structure-based prediction of eukaryotic protein phosphorylation sites. Journal of Molecular Biology: 294(5): 1351-1362, 1999.

Pfam

R.D. Finn, A. Bateman, J. Clements, P. Coggill, R.Y. Eberhardt, S.R. Eddy, A. Heger, K. Hetherington, L. Holm, J. Mistry, E.L.L. Sonnhammer, J. Tate, M. Punta. The Pfam protein families database Nucleic Acids Research (2014) Database Issue 42:D222-D230

Uniprot

The UniProt Consortium. Activities at the Universal Protein Resource (UniProt) Nucleic Acids Res. 42: D191-D198 (2014).
