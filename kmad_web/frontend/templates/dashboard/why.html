Why did we develop KMAD?
The reason for developing KMAD was our opinion that sometimes, in case of disordered (and less often, but structured as well) proteins plain amino acid sequence is not enough to create a biologically relevant sequence alignment. Here is a very simple example to visualize the problem that we want deal with (red residues are phosphoserines):

good alignment:

The first alignment is what all methods that don't take into account any other information than amino acid sequence would produce and in terms of the amino acid of the amino acid similarity it is the most optimal one. However, we should remember that the best alignment is the one that represents the biology the best, and not the one that is the best solution to a mathematical equation.
