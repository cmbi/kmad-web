R157S

disease: Non-syndromic orofacial cleft 5

PTMs
Corresponding residue in mouse was found to be di-methylated. (PhosphoSite)
motif: TRG_NLS_Bipartite_1
regex: [KR][KR].{7,15}[^DE]((K[RK])|(RK))(([^DE][KR])|([KR][^DE]))[^DE]
matching sequence segment:RRLSPPACTLRKHKTNRKPRT
compartment: nucleus
Description
  • Nuclear Localization Signal, recognized by importin α, transporting proteins to the nucleus
  • probably most proteins functioning in the nucleus have an NLS:
    "Proteins that enter the nucleus in preformed complexes may not require their own targeting motif (Dingwall,1982). Nevertheless, it is clear that most proteins do specify their own import signal and, of these, the vast majority have an NLS that binds to importin-alpha (also termed karyopherin-alpha)." (from the ELM description)
  • Green residues are recognized by one the two binding sites of importin α. Some proteins only have the green part of the motif (TRG_NLS_MonoCore_2, TRG_NLS_MonoExtN_4 - slightly different regex, but also matches this segment: (([PKR].{0,1}[^DE])|([PKR]))((K[RK])|(RK))(([^DE][KR])|([KR][^DE]))[^DE] )
  • The Bipartite version of the motif is bound to both NLS sites from importin α. The N-terminal RR part is bound in the so-called minor groove, while the longer C-terminal stretch in the major groove.
  • "The additional binding energy contributed by the upstream cluster of basic resdiues relaxes the requirements for the downstream monopartite-like sequences" (Hodel, et al., 2001)
  • "The binding affinity of this sequence for ΔIBB importin α increases from 3 μM for the monopartite sequence to 13.5 nM for the bipartite variant." (which is from around 7,5 kcal/mol to around 10.7 kcal/mol)
  • "The addition of two basic residues upstream of the monopartite sequence (...) contributed an average of ~3.1 kcal/mol to the binding affinity"
  • "there is a complicated but monotonic quantitative relationship between the affinity of an NLS for the import receptor, importin α, and the steady-state accumulation of the cargo in the nucleus." (Hodel, et al., 2006) RN/C (ratio of the nuclear to the cytoplasmic concentration) increased around two-fold for the sequences with the bipartite motif.
Hypothesis
Disappearance of the N-terminal part of the motif could weaken the interaction with importin α and thus decrease the transport rate of the MSX1 protein to the nucleus. As the Hodel stody shows the two basic residues do significantly increase the binding energy.
Kamamoto et al. tested whether the mutation disrupts the NLS and observed that the mutated protein was still transported to the nucleus.
However, this does not undermine the hypothesis - the localisation of the wild type and mutated proteins was only checked after 48 hours, so it does not tell us anything about the rates of transport.
