BAliBASE

Correct motif alignment is an extremely difficult task to perform, even by manual curation. There are situations, where the 'correct' solution is not obvious or it does not exist at all. Therefore, it comes to us as no surprise that there are alignments in the BAliBASE benchmark where ultimate solutions seem euqally correct. We provide here several examples (along with two examples from BAliBASE for structured proteins) to illustrate ambiguity that is intrinsic to protein alignment.
Two of these problems were explained to us by one of the BAliBASE creators, prof. Julie Thompson. (We include the answers in the text)
We found many examples where the BAliBASE creators overruled the ELM motif assignment. Even though KMAD often agrees with BAliBASE curators one should actually manually validate all cases where it does not. In summary, we believe that problems associated with annotation and curation are responsible for a few percent of the scores. Given that the methods tested differ by at most a few percent too we have to conclude that all methods tested perform equally well considering this margin of error.

Most of the following alignments are BAliBASE reference alignments (unless stated otherwise), where the bright orange residues are residues included in the TP/FN motif instances by BAliBASE, and purple residues are the segments that match the ELM pattern.

How is it determined which motifs are true positives?
Alignment BOX230 from the reference 9, subset RV921
Motif: LIG_FHA_2
Regex: "..(T)..[DE]."

1.The LIG_FHA_2 motif is annotated only in the first sequence, and it is predicted in the other sequences. When there are multiple occurences of the motif in other sequences how is it decided which of them should be aligned with the one in the top sequence?
2. Why is the motif in the 5th sequence shifted to the left? If we assume that all of them are true positives, shouldn't they all be lined up?
3. Concerning the false negative motif in the 9th sequence. Why do you assume that the prediction (or actually lack of prediction) is wrong and it's not just a case of a motif changing it's position? Motifs very often 'move around' in the disordered regions.

Answers:

1. We chose the motif that is aligned with the annotated sequence based on the sequence conservation score of the motifs and their relative positions in the sequences with respect to other conserved blocks. For example, the annotated motif is ESTDEES. For Q6GR07, there are 2 possible motifs: GSTDEEQ and SKTGKEH, that appear close to the annotated motif in the alignment. We chose GSTDEEQ because it has a higher sequence identity with the annotated motif.
2. The motif defined in ELM is ..(T)..[DE], so in Q1DTN6, there are 2 overlapping occurrences of the motif: DETATES and TATESDR. We chose the second one to avoid introducing a new gap in the alignment that was not just by the sequence identity with the first motif.

BBS12034

Alignment BB12034 from reference 1 (RV912)

BAliBASE is said to use SAP structure alignment as the base for the sequence alignment, but there are some discrepancies between the SAP and BAliBASE alignments. T-Coffee Expresso uses SAP to produce a structure based alignment and the result is also different then the reference alignment. For example the "DRTG" segment is at position 33 in the Expresso and SAP alignments, while in the reference alignment it is shifted to the left.
1. Why was it decided that this segment should be shifted to the left, although it is aligned differently in the structure alignment?

Answer:

The SAP alignments were manually refined to align residues with known functions. In this case, in 1aiq, the motif DRTG contains a residue R in contact with a ligand (according to the pdb sum website). The same function is defined for 1b02 in the motif DVRTK.


While we agree that the two arginines in contact with a ligand should be aligned lined up together it is still not clear to us why aren't they includeed in the coreblocks. We have knowledge about their importance and about them being equivalent, but it is not penalized if a method does not align them properly.

RV913_BOX158

This example shows that BAliBASE motif score might not always be a relevant measure of motif alignment. The motif that BAliBASE scores for here is a KDEL motif, described by regular expression: "[KRHQSAP][DENQT]EL$". Here we present 3 alignment excerpts, with motif scores 0.37, 0.88, and 0.78. Each alignment is shown in two versions - with BAliBASE motifs highlighted (orange) and with KDEL pattern matches highlighted (purple). In the alignment 1, which gets the worst score, the actual KDEL motifs are aligned perfectly. The best scoring one - alignment 2 aligns incorrectly one residue. In alignment 3, which also gets quite a good score, the motif residues are aligned with each other, but the whole motif is so spread out because of the gaps that it is hardly possible for it to be biologically relevant.
There is no evidence of KDEL (or KDEL equivalent) motif not being at the very end of the sequence. Thus, the 'FN' motifs should not be used as an 'arbitrary truth' for the benchmarking purposes.
In one of the sequences BAliBASE annotated the motif as a 3-residue segment ("DEL"), but we suppose it was not done on purpose.

RV913_BOX146

The motif annotated here is a clathrin box (ELM ID: LIG_Clathr_ClatBox_1). Its regular expression is "[IVLMF].[IVLMF][DE]" The motif pattern matches the fourth sequence in a different location than annotated by BAliBASE - much further from the C-terminus. Obviously one would not like to use this outlying motif for the motif score, but it is not fair to give penalty points for a an alignment of a motif that is not there. Although BAliBASE alignment is in this case most probably correct, alignment of this segment should in our opinion be included only in the general score and not in the motif score.

RV913_BOX082

The motif in this example is an SH2 ligand (ELM ID: LIG_SH2_STAT5, regex: "(Y)[VLTFIC].."). The pattern does not match the segment in the last sequence.
This example represents another problem with the BAliBASE motif benchmark - if there are several instances of a motif in an alignment it only scores for one instance. Thus an alignment that would get two of the motif columns aligned right but the rightmost column wrong, would get a lower score than an alignment where only the last column is well aligned.
