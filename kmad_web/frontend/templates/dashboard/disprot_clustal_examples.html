We encountered several types of errors in Clustal Omega alignments, that are listed here with representative examples for each kind.

Terminal motifs

60S acidic ribosomal protein P1-B
In this example the turquoise motif LIG_BIR_II_1 at the N-terminus is clearly conserved, but is not very well aligned in the Clustal Omega alignment. KMAD lines up all of its instances together. An alignment like this tells us more about the reliability of the motif prediction.

In the KMAD alignment the motifs form the left side are shifted to the right, which shows how well the motif is conserved. Eukaryotic translation initiation factor 4E-binding protein 1
This is another example of an incorrectly aligned LIG_BIR_II_1 motif. In the KMAD alignment only in the 6th sequence the moitf is not lined up with the conserved column - but this is actually a desired result. Aligning it with all the other instances of the motif would break other similarities. For exmaple the identity of the 5 residue segment (positions 3 - 7) between sequences 6 and 3 or 4 is 80%, but if the motif from the 6th sequence started on position 3 like in other sequences this identity would drop down to 40%.

Other motifs

Kappa-casein
DEG_SCF_FBW7_1 highlighted in neon green is split up in two parts in the Clustal Omega alignment. Such an alignment could lead to an (most probably) incorrect conclusion that the 'PTTE' segment in the lower sequences is an insertion. The KMAD alignment doesn't align the motif perfectly, because the similarities in the segment on positions 151 - 158 are too strong, therefore forcing the motif to be lined up even more would decrease the quality of the alignment.

Myelin basic protein
In this example at first sight it seems that the MOD_PKA_2 motif (purple) should be shifted by 6 residues to the right to be aligned with the conserved column having the same motif in other sequences. The similarity amino acid-wise would not be increased but at leats the motif would be aligned well. But when we look at the KMAD alignment we can see that the mistake Clustal made is even worse - KMAD shifts the whole 'GRDSRS' segment to the right side of the gap, where it fits perfectly, having 100% identity with the first 8 sequences. We show the same examples in the PTMs section, as the KMAD alignment also improves PTM alignment in these sequences.

Bone sialoprotein 1
Here Clustal Omega made several mistakes. First, the phosphorylated serines in the first sequence at position 3919 and 3920 are shifted one residue to the left relative to the column of conserved phosphorylated serines from other sequences. Second, the phosphorylated serines in sequences 1 - 5 between positions 3951 and 3958, could be all lined up together, especially considering their very similar, low complexity, "N,D,Q,E-rich" surrounding. Last but not least, N-glycosylated asparagines at position 3985 in sequences 1-3 and at position 3983 in the CLustal alignment are lined up together in the KMAD alignment.

Where KMAD goes wrong
Eukaryotic translation initiation factor 4E-binding protein 1
Here Clustal better aligns the diverse region in sequences 7 - 9. The cascading 'LKE' segments are an unfortunate side effect of the profile alignment. Some mistakes like that are unavoidable - just as errors introduced by Clustal that are a result of not removing gaps that have been once introduced. All of the heuristic MSA algorithms have their flaws, and none of them will always produce a good result.

Clustal Omega

KMAD
Non-histone chromosomal protein HMG-14
In this case KMAD aligns the short 'SAD' segment worse, because of the different substitution matrix that it uses - the IDP specific matrix. Substitution from A to S gives a much higher score, than in the BLOSUM or PAM matrices - that is why aligning the phosphoserine at position 6 to a column of alanines and two serines with predicted phosphorylation gives a higher score than aligning it at position 7, although there are two annotated phosphoserines.
