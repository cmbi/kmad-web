Yeast 60S acidic ribosomal protein P1-B (RLA3_CANAX)

This example shows how KMAD can improve motif alignment. The motif LIG_TRAF2_2 highlighted in orange (in sequence 1 on positions 17 - 20, 85 - 88, 90 - 93, and 94 - 97) seems to be conserved in around position 85 but is not very well aligned. In the KMAD alignment more instances of this motif are lined up together. In some of the sequences motif's location and surrounding deviates too much for the SLiM score to overcome the alignment. Other motifs in the alignment are not always lined up together, but this is not considered a disadvantage - KMAD's purpose is to align the motifs only if the rets of the alignment stays intact. If the motif weight was increased the instances of the same motif would be 'forced' to be aligned together, but it would not be beneficial for the alignment quality.
